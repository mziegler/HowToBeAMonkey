The capuchins of the Lomas Barbudal Monkey Project have become one of the most widely-studied wild monkey populations in the world. Long-term field studies give us important insight into primate conservation, ecology, evolution, and human origins. We have to act quick, because the monkeys' tropical forest habitats are vanishing at an alarming rate!

The Capuchin Foundation supports the scientific, conservation, and education work of the monkey project. Funding for long-term primate research projects is hard to come by these days, and every little bit helps. The Capuchin Foundation needs donations to cover the costs of equipment, feeding our mostly-volunteer research crew, and putting together a massive database of the project's historical data. You can help!
